Which features you need to consider when purchasing a drone?

Flying Times: It is the average flying time of a drone with one single charge. A drone for beginners has its flying time from 5 to 10 minutes. Professional one often has more than 20 minutes. Extra battery is an option you can extend its flying time.

Signal Range: It indicates how far a drone can fly away from your controller. The range of indoor mini-drones has less than 30m while the professional outdoor one has nearly 5km.

Auto Home: It is an option that a drone automatically lands on the location specified using GPS. For example, when a drone is out of the signal range, it can be set to come back to the position where it takes off.

Camera: Many of you began interested in drones after watching video clips recorded from drone. It is a great option to have. There are two types of camera options for drones: one is an on-board camera option, and the other is a mountable camera option which your own camera can be attached to the drone.

Controller: Some drones come without a controller, which means you need to have your own controller. A smartphone is one option, of course, after installing a control app into your smartphone. Although the smartphone option is easy to learn and use, it has a short signal range and gets interfered easily due to its Wi-Fi based operation.
